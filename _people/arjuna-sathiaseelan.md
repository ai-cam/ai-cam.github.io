biography: Dr. Arjuna Sathiaseelan is a Visiting Fellow and Technical Lead at
  the Frugal AI Hub, Cambridge Judge Business School, and CEO & Co-Founder
  of Flipped.ai. Previously, he directed the Networking for Development (N4D
  Lab) at the University of Cambridge's Computer Laboratory, focusing on
  connecting the next three billion internet users. He is also the founder of
  Saving Voices, an initiative to preserve indigenous languages through AI voice
  models. He founded and chaired the IRTF Global Access to the Internet for All
  (GAIA) research group and served on the Internet Research Steering Group. He
  has advised organisations including the UN Foundation's $75 million Digital
  Impact Alliance. He is also a member of the Center for Science and Policy
  (CSaP) and the Cambridge Big Data initiative.He holds a PhD, MSc in Computing
  and Internet Systems from King's College London, and a Bachelor's in Computer
  Science from NIT Trichy, India. He is a recipient of the King's International
  Impact Award (2026) from Kings College London and the Young Alumni Achiever
  Award from NIT Trichy (2016)
